Welcome to the accounting department!

Your accountants identify promising investments by assigning a price to every resource, using these prices to calculate the costs and benefits of available investments.

To learn more about the individual accounting tables, hover your paw over them.

Alas, your accountants are not omniscient. In particular, they do not consider:

  • science storage
  • synergies from buying science or storage
  • benefits provided by your leader
  • unicorn religion beyond sun spires
  • metaphysics beyond early price reduction upgrades
  • investment opportunities beyond yarn

The production table shows resource production and prices

The columns mean, in order:

  • conversion activity slider (for converted resources)
  • estimated net production per second (not including resources spent in conversion)
  • the name of the resource
  • the current price for that resource
    (left-click to increase, right-click to decrease)
  • whether this rare resource is available

For resources produced by workers, your accountants automatically determine the price based on worker productivity (each worker earns 1 per second; if a woodcutter cuts 0.2 wood per second, the wood price is set to 1/0.2 = 5). The price of coal is determined by the productivity of your geologists, but may be reduced manually in case you don't have geologists yet. Likewise, faith price may be reduced if you don't have priests.

For resources created through a conversion process, you can inspect conversion costs and benefits by hovering your paw over the resource.

For resources converted on command (crafting, trading, or similar activities), your accountants suggest a resource price based on the cost of raw materials and other outputs. You may adjust this price if you obtain them from a different source (such as getting blueprints by trading rather than crafting them). For resources converted on command, the conversion activitiy slider sets the proportion of raw materials converted. For instance, for fur, it sets the proportion of catpower spent hunting (your accountants need to know this value so they can calculate the benefit of conversion upgrades such as hunting armor).

You can indicate the availability of fur, ivory, and unicorns by clicking the right-most column. If you hover your paw over that indicator, you can see the benefit and cost of making these resources available for consumption.

This table shows all currently available investment opportunities, in other of efficiency.

The columns mean:

  • break even point = number of seconds until the investment has paid for itself
  • Name of the investment
  • level of the investement (or R for a researched upgrade)

Investments in gray require additional research not included in the efficiency assessment.

Hover your paw over an investment to see a breakdown of investment cost and benefits.

Left click to implement an investment, right click to undo, or click the level to edit the value directly.

Note that the benefit of price reduction upgrades is currently estimated at 0, as they have no effect on ongoing production.

This table shows investment opportunities for increasing storage. It works much like the ordinary investment table, except that a break even can not be calculated. Instead, the cost of storage extensions is added to investments that require it.

Note that buildings that also have other effects are not yet listed here, because it would be unclear to what extend their cost is already paid for by their other effects. As a result, your accountants assume that science storage is unlimited, and don't suggest building oil wells to increase oil storage.
